Info on old poorly documented computer parts: How to fix your old broken computer

This weekend I shipped out my old desktop and had to figure out what was wrong with it. Not only was it broken before, I also had taken it apart and damaged parts.

Sound like something you might do? Let's talk through all of the problems I had to solve.

Instructions for the careful: Something is probably broken.

Let's start easy. It's an old computer. Your HDD is likely going to be corrupted. Fix that. On another computer.

Next. Power Supply. Get a cheap new one and see if that was the problem. You can do this for 40$. If it's not the problem, keep it.

I assume your HDD and PSU were both trashed. Mine were. This still wasn't my problem, though replacing them now allowed my computer to primary boot.

Did you upgrade your BIOS? Is your flash drive partitioned correctly? Your Hard Drive? Are all external USB devices unplugged?

You're careful - at least now. You feel as though you checked everything. Things are not acting how they are supposed to - they are not acting like others' problems either.

But your computer is still not working. Here is my useful information, that I have never seen online.

Instructions for the careless: You probably broke something.

I knew my problem had something to do with my Motherboard or CPU. In fact, it almost seemed to be both.

In my case, a long time ago, when trying to repair the pc, I dropped something on the pins where my CPU is seated.

Upon inspecting my pins, I noticed some were bent - and touching! This (of course) is bad.

Using a small tool, I was able to bend the pins back into place. I have had no trouble since.
